܀ ⚘ ܀ ⚘ ܀

i had a different entry planned for today: some meditation on walking, observing, and living slowly; on how good things – like friendship, relationships, and a sense of home – take time.

but, as it does every twenty-eight or so days, my brain had its own ideas. so i thought i'd give it space to run and let it tire itself out. here are the thoughts that lapped my mind this morning.

܀ ⚘ ܀ ⚘ ܀

i hate that the person i want to see the most is 2,600 miles away.

i hate that they are living 3 hours in the future.

i hate time zones.

i hate that the distance doesn't matter, really, because anxiety would squirrel its way into my mind even if we were in the same room.

i hate that it's already happened.

i hate how quickly i latch onto someone.

i hate that i know i'm just latching onto the idea of them.

i hate that when i first read attached, i didn't stop crying for two days and couldn't pick it back up for three weeks.

i hate that i've already found myself in the first ten pages of my new book, ripe: "i read the articles, i keep up with the research, i wait for the day discovery will make sense of the black hole living alongside me."

i hate that no one in my life gets what i mean when i ask if they ever feel a weird darkness when they touch the space right below their rib cage.

i hate that my sadness is the loneliest thing about me, and the most common.

i hate that i have my best creative ideas when i feel the most manic.

i hate that i love being a little manic.

i hate that i need good, consistent sleep to be my highest functioning self – but it's impossible to attain when my mind is on one.

i hate that i need to exhaust my body to let my brain know everything's okay.

i hate that this subjects my body to injury, and being injured means i have to rest.

i hate that resting gives my mind all this time and space to catch up.

i hate that i know what will meant now.

i hate that things with him imploded rather than conclude.

i hate that that's a pattern i'm familiar with.

i hate that when i was an intern, a co-worker ten years older took me out for drinks and pool. then, tipsy at our office, he slid his hand on my knee and said something that still knots the muscles between my shoulders.

i hate that he never acknowledged me at work after that.

i hate that i can't listen to my favorite band without the image of his freckled hands intruding.

i hate that i have more memories like this than i can count with my fingers.

i hate that i know all these methods to regulate my nervous system – inhaling deeply, then sharply again at the top, and letting it all out slowly; splashing cold water on my face; noticing 5 things i see, 4 things i feel, 3 things i hear, 2 things i smell, 1 thing i taste – but my brain knows them, too.

i hate that my brain can anticipate these tricks up my mammalian sleeve, and huffs at their futility.

i hate that i do not fight. i do not flee. i freeze.

i hate that i have ten million – really, three or four – things i need to take care of that i've been avoiding all week.

i hate that i turn to claude to hold my hand when i get overwhelmed.

i hate that i use it now to outsource my most human feelings.

i hate that i have prompted: "<insert sympathetic comment>"

i hate that we all know what we're contributing to and not doing shit to curb it.

i hate that i know what i'm contributing to and not doing shit to curb it.

i hate that i've worked in tech for ten years in all these "social good" spaces – education, mental health, now democracy – and all i have to show is my jadedness about what good we can actually do.

i hate that my work pays me well, but not well enough to not worry about not working.

i hate that i cosplay someone who still cares.

i hate that i feel guilty about not caring more, and that that's what's keeping me here.

i hate that, after this, i don't know what i'm good for.

i hate that healthcare is tied to having a job in america.

i hate that i don't know where to go from here.

i hate that i have to keep going anyway.

܀ ⚘ ܀ ⚘ ܀

this all occurs within the span of maybe 3 minutes. my brain does not tire.

the cat & dog i'm pet-sitting make themselves and their hunger known, so my mind is given a break. caring for something else takes me out of myself and gives my brain the thing it craves – a small, simple step: get up. feed them. feed yourself.
